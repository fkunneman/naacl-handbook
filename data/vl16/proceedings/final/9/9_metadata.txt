Abstract#==#In this paper, we investigate whether a neural network model can learn the
meaning of natural language quantifiers (no, some and all) from their use in
visual contexts. We show that memory networks perform well in this task, and 
that explicit counting is not necessary to the system's performance, supporting
psycholinguistic evidence on the acquisition of quantifiers.
