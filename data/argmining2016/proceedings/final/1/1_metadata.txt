Abstract#==#In this paper, we introduce an approach for recognizing the absence of opposing
arguments in persuasive essays. We model this task as a binary document
classification and show that adversative transitions in combination with
unigrams and syntactic production rules significantly outperform a challenging
heuristic baseline. Our approach yields an accuracy of 75.6% and 84% of human
performance in a persuasive essay corpus with various topics.
