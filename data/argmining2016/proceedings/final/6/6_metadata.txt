Abstract#==#This paper focuses on the automated extraction of argument components from user
content in the German online participation project Tempelhofer Feld. We adapt
existing argumentation models into a new model for decision-oriented online
participation. Our model consists of three categories: major positions,
claims, and premises. We create a new German corpus for argument mining by
annotating our dataset with our model. Afterwards, we focus on the two
classification tasks of identifying argumentative sentences and predicting
argument components in sentences. We achieve macro-averaged
F_1 measures of 69.77% and 68.5%, respectively.
