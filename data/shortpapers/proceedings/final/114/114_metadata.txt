Abstract#==#Phrase table pruning is the act of removing phrase pairs from a
phrase table to make it smaller, ideally removing the least useful
phrases first.
 We propose a phrase table pruning method that formulates the task as a
 submodular function maximization problem,
 and solves it by using a greedy heuristic algorithm. 
 The proposed method can scale with input size              and long phrases, and
 experiments show that it achieves higher BLEU scores than
 state-of-the-art pruning methods.
